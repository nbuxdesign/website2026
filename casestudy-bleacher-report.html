Bleacher Report / Warner Bros. Discovery

Taking a sports product from 1.0 to 2.0 without losing what made it fast.

Bleacher Report already had a clear product voice: fast, current, fan-first, and built around sports culture. My work was to modernize the web and app experience without sanding that edge down.

I worked as a Senior UX Designer for four years with product, engineering, editorial, brand, and design system partners inside Warner Bros. Discovery Sports.

The work was bigger than a redesign.

On paper, this was a redesign of the Bleacher Report website and app. In practice, it was a product maturity project: new surfaces, tight timelines, a large team, and a design system built for daily product work. That changed how the team made decisions as much as how the interface looked.

The useful work was turning unclear product ideas into flows, flows into decisions, and decisions into front-end-ready design. The goal was to make the next step clear for designers, engineers, product partners, and stakeholders.

My role was to keep the work clear and moving.

I worked across concept development, interaction design, presentation, and production design. Some projects were mine to drive; others meant joining a larger effort, finding the missing structure, and making the system easier to use. The work was strongest when complexity became legible before it reached implementation.

Sports adds pressure: live moments, editorial urgency, personalization, platform differences, and fan behavior all create edge cases. The work had to be direct enough for fast use and strong enough to hold at scale. That meant designing for normal states, urgent states, and the awkward states between them.

What I was responsible for

  • Developed end-to-end flows for new product concepts
  • Designed app experiences across iOS and Android
  • Led UX for the bleacherreport.com redesign
  • Presented concepts and rationale to cross-functional teams
  • Worked with product, engineering, editorial, brand, and design-system partners
  • Contributed to BR Stadium, the design system adopted across WBD Sports

A design system that had to survive real product work.

BR Stadium mattered because the redesign needed more than new screens. It needed reusable decisions: components, patterns, platform behaviors, and enough structure for designers to move quickly.

My contribution was shaping product work so the system became clearer and more useful. A good design system should reduce unnecessary negotiation while leaving room for product thinking.

Redesign should preserve what works, then give it a stronger system.

The website redesign was the clearest expression of the shift.

Leading bleacherreport.com meant treating the web as its own product, not a mirror of the app. The site had to support editorial velocity, carry the BR voice, and stay easy to scan.

The work moved from product framing into page structures, interaction patterns, and final UI. The goal was a web experience that felt current without slowing people down.

What shipped

The program shipped BR 2.0 across iOS and Android and the redesigned bleacherreport.com. The site later won a Webby Award for Best Sports Website, a public marker for practical product work.
